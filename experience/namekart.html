🚀 My Interview Experience at Namekart – iOS Developer Intern

Off-Campus | 3rd Year, 2nd Semester | Final Year Computer Engineering Student

During my third year (second semester), I appeared for an off-campus interview process with Namekart for the position of iOS Application Developer Intern.

1️⃣ Shortlisting & Online Test

I was shortlisted by the company and received a test link to appear for the first round. The test focused on general iOS development knowledge, logical thinking, and coding basics.

2️⃣ Technical Interview

After successfully completing the test, I received an email for the next round: the Technical Interview. Topics covered included:

  • ✅ iOS basics (Swift, View lifecycle, UIKit/SwiftUI)
  • ✅ Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) concepts
  • ✅ Data Structures (Array, Linked List, String manipulation)

The interviewer assessed both my coding logic and fundamental understanding of development principles.

3️⃣ HR Round

I reached the HR round, where we discussed my academic journey and internship expectations. However, the company was specifically looking for final-year students who had already completed their degree.

🚫 Outcome

Since I was still in the third year at that time, I wasn’t selected. However, reaching this stage gave me valuable experience and boosted my confidence.

💡 Takeaway

This opportunity helped me understand the structure of a real interview process: test → technical → HR. I realized the importance of DSA, OOP concepts, and iOS fundamentals in interviews.

Advice for students: Keep practicing iOS concepts, DSA, and mock interviews — even if you don’t meet all eligibility criteria now, you’ll be ready when the time comes! 💪📱
