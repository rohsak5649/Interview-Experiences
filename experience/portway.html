📝 My Interview Experience at Portway International

📍 Campus Placement | DY Patil Institute of Technology, Pimpri

Portway International came to our college for a campus placement drive. The process started with two offline, pen-paper based rounds:

🧪 Step 1: Aptitude Test (Offline)

The first round was an aptitude test conducted in-person. Around 140 students appeared for this round.

🧪 Step 2: Python Test (Offline)

After the aptitude test, a Python programming test was conducted, again on paper. Based on both test performances, only 5 students were shortlisted for the interview round. I was proud to be one of them.

🗣️ Technical Interview

The interview was conducted in-person at our own campus. The panel asked a variety of questions:

  • Detailed explanation of my project
  • Questions on MySQL and database concepts
  • SQLite-related queries
  • Node.js and Express.js – some technical questions
  • Two coding problems (which I solved correctly)

📉 Final Outcome

Although I answered about 80% of the questions correctly, I was not selected for the final HR round. From the 5 shortlisted students, only 1 was selected to proceed further.

I still don’t know the exact reason for not being selected, but I took it positively and used it as motivation to improve further.

💡 Learning

This experience taught me that clearing written and even technical interviews doesn’t always guarantee a selection. Sometimes companies have very limited seats, or are looking for a very specific skill set. Still, I gave my best and it boosted my confidence in facing offline interviews and paper-based coding rounds.
