🚀 My Interview Experience with Jar (iOS Internship)

Off-Campus | Final Year Computer Engineering Student | iOS Developer Intern

1️⃣ Application Process

I applied off-campus, and after my resume was shortlisted, I received a phone call from HR to discuss my application.

2️⃣ Live Coding Round (Online)

This round was conducted online. I was given a buggy code and had to:

  • Debug the code
  • Fix all issues
  • Push the corrected version to GitHub
  • Submit within 1 hour 30 minutes

✅ I successfully cleared this round!

3️⃣ Technical Interview

I received a call from HR two hours later informing me that I was selected for the technical round. The interview was challenging and covered:

  • ✅ Swift Basics
  • ✅ MVVM Architecture
  • ✅ OOP Concepts
  • ✅ Stacks & Data Structures
  • ✅ Difference between Class & Struct
  • ✅ Friend Functions
  • ✅ Nil & Optional Values
  • ✅ My iOS Projects
  • ✅ API Integration & Firebase
  • ✅ SwiftUI (major focus!)
  • ✅ Extensions in Swift

❌ I couldn’t answer a few questions confidently, which led to my rejection in this round.

4️⃣ Final HR Round (Not Qualified)

Since I didn’t clear the technical round, I wasn’t eligible for the HR round. However:

  • 💡 I was among only 5 candidates who cleared the live coding round.
  • 🧑‍🎓 All others were graduates — I’m still in my final year!

💡 Takeaways

This was a fantastic learning experience for me. Although I didn’t make it to the final round, I gained:

  • 🎯 Confidence in my Swift & SwiftUI skills
  • 📈 Motivation to keep learning and improving

To all aspiring iOS developers: Keep learning, keep coding, and never be afraid of challenges! 🚀
