🚀 My Interview Experience at Xalta – Automation Software Intern Role

Campus Referral | Computer Engineering Department | Final Year

The opportunity with Xalta came through my college’s Training and Placement Department, which forwarded my resume directly to the company’s HR team along with a few other students from our Computer Department.

🧩 Interview Experience

I was shortlisted for the direct interview round. Here's how it went:

1️⃣ Object-Oriented Programming (OOP)

The interviewer started with simple OOP questions, focusing on core concepts. I was also asked to:

  • ✅ Create a class
  • ✅ Perform basic operations on objects
  • ✅ Explain concepts like encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism

2️⃣ MySQL

The next set of questions involved SQL queries and theoretical database concepts. Topics included:

  • 🔍 What is a cursor in SQL?
  • 🔍 What is an index and how does it help?
  • 🧠 Writing basic SQL queries

3️⃣ Array Logic Problem

Then I was given an interesting array question:

Given an array that stores only 1 and -1, arrange or maintain it in such a way that the sum of all elements becomes 0.

I attempted this problem using logic-based reasoning to balance the count of 1’s and -1’s.

4️⃣ Python Questions

In the final part of the interview, the interviewer asked me a few Python-related questions. However, since I am not proficient in Python, I couldn’t answer them effectively.

🚫 Outcome

Although I did well in OOP, MySQL, and logic problems, my lack of experience in Python affected my performance in the final section.

💡 Takeaway

This interview taught me the importance of language flexibility and being prepared for multi-language questions in interviews. I plan to explore Python basics moving forward, while continuing to strengthen my OOP and problem-solving skills.

Tip for juniors: Focus not only on your preferred language but also be open to other common industry languages like Python and Java. It can increase your chances in technical interviews!
