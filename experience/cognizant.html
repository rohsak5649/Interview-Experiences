🚀 My Interview Experience at Cognizant – Campus Placement

On-Campus | Final Year Computer Engineering | Cognizant Software Role

Cognizant visited our college for campus placements, and I was excited to participate in the process. Here's my experience through each stage of the selection:

1️⃣ Screening Round

I cleared the initial **screening round**, which was used to shortlist candidates based on academic records and eligibility.

2️⃣ Communication Round

This round was focused on testing English comprehension, grammar, and communication fluency. I cleared this round successfully.

3️⃣ Online Assessment

The assessment round was quite comprehensive and included:

  • ✅ 2 Coding Problems
  • ✅ 2 MySQL Queries
  • ✅ 1 Frontend Task using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

I was able to solve almost 90% of this round, which helped me get shortlisted for the interview.

4️⃣ Technical + HR Interview

The interview panel asked me about my preferred programming language. They gave me the choice between Java and Python, but I confidently told them that I specialize in C++.

They asked me questions from both **Java** and **C++**, covering topics like:

  • ✅ OOPS Concepts – Inheritance, Polymorphism, Overloading & Overriding
  • ✅ Differences between Class, Object, and Struct
  • ✅ Code writing for Arrays and Strings
  • ✅ Basic and intermediate DSA problems
  • ✅ SQL – I confidently answered all questions and scored a perfect 100/100

🚫 Outcome

Although I did well in MySQL and general programming, I couldn’t answer some Java-based and DSA questions due to less familiarity. Since the company required strength in Java, I wasn’t selected in the final round.

💡 Takeaway

This experience taught me how crucial it is to be flexible with programming languages in company-specific scenarios. While I was confident in C++, the interviewers expected some level of Java proficiency.

Advice: If you're preparing for product-based or service-based companies like Cognizant, make sure to:

  • 📘 Know OOPS deeply (across multiple languages)
  • 📚 Practice DSA and SQL consistently
  • 🧠 Be ready for frontend and backend assessments

Though I didn’t get selected, this interview experience boosted my confidence and clarified where I need to improve. 🚀
